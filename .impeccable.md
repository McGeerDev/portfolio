## Design Context

### Users
Hiring managers evaluating for SRE and software engineering roles. They scan quickly for company names, titles, and tech competence. They're reviewing multiple candidates — clarity and speed matter.

### Brand Personality
Warm, grounded, honest. Direct without being cold. The portfolio reflects someone who values people and craftsmanship.

### Aesthetic Direction
Minimalist typographic design. Lekton (body) and Lexend Zetta (display) with heavy letter-spacing. Warm oklch neutrals — no pure black or white. Light mode only. The typography does the heavy lifting; decoration is absent by design.

Anti-references: card grids, timeline dots/lines, icon-per-section layouts, glassmorphism, gradient text, dark mode with glowing accents, anything that reads as "AI portfolio template."

### Design Principles
1. **Scannable in 10 seconds** — key information (names, titles, companies) must be immediately visible through typographic hierarchy alone
2. **Let whitespace breathe** — generous spacing between sections, tight grouping within related items
3. **Pragmatic over decorative** — every element earns its place; if it doesn't aid comprehension, it's cut
4. **Consistent vocabulary** — reuse the same heading styles, spacing rhythm, and color tokens across all pages
5. **Warm, not sterile** — tinted neutrals, readable body text, human language
